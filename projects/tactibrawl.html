Tactibrawl is a pixel-art tactical brawler where two players place heroes on a grid and battle for arena supremacy. The design challenge: make turn-based tactics feel exciting, readable, and accessible without losing the fantasy depth.

I designed the full game UI in Figma — from the splash screen and loading sequence through to hero selection, the tutorial system, and the battle arena. The characters were hand-drawn in pixel art style. Environments were AI-generated and art-directed to match the fantasy aesthetic.

The whole game experience was prototyped as an interactive Figma file — fully playable, with transitions and tutorial dialogue.

Process

HOW THE
GAME WAS
BUILT.

  • 01
    Game Concept + UX Flow
    Mapped the full game flow from title screen to battle end. Defined the core loop: splash → hero select → tutorial → place on grid → battle. Every screen had a clear purpose and transition.
  • 02
    Art Direction — Pixel Fantasy
    Established the visual language: pixel art characters on AI-generated painterly environments. Gold as the primary accent — matches the game's fantasy register without going generic RPG.
  • 03
    Character Design — Hand-Drawn Pixel Art
    Characters were hand-drawn in pixel art style — Tank, Ranger, Mage, and Sola the guide. Each character needed distinct silhouettes readable at small sizes on the battle grid.
  • 04
    Environment Design — AI-Assisted
    Backgrounds were AI-generated and heavily art-directed: deep forest arena, waterfall canyon, indoor training room. Each scene was selected and refined to match the pixel art tone without clashing.
  • 05
    Tutorial System — Sola
    Designed a dialogue-driven tutorial with Sola as the guide character. Multi-expression sprite sheet (neutral, surprised, happy, stern) reacts to tutorial context. Dialogue boxes styled as classic RPG parchment.
  • 06
    Full Prototype in Figma
    Every screen wired up with transitions. Fully interactive — click through the full game experience from title to battle without needing to build it in code first.
